## Designing a Seamless Account Experience for Rental Living

Timeline: 3 Months
Role: UX Researcher, Visual Designer, Collaboration
Tools: Figma, GPT, Whimsical
Team: Designer: Tanya (Primary), Vikram (Mentor). Collaborators: Product Managers, Developers

Overview: Rentickle's account section suffered from redundant, poorly hierarchized data and a document upload process that ran entirely through manual email exchanges. Navigation gave no clear sense of purpose, and critical areas like KYC and document handling lacked any real guidance.

Design Process: Followed an Observe, Orient, Decide, Act structure. Observe: gathered direct feedback from stakeholders and customers on approval delays and unclear order status. Orient: ran competitor research across onboarding, UX flow, service, and payment patterns to see what other platforms did well. Decide: mapped the account section's structure and prioritized fixes into Must Have versus Should Have. Act: translated the structure into a mobile-first design, then built a parallel desktop experience for users who preferred managing rentals on larger screens.

What Shipped: A step-by-step KYC flow with instant e-KYC and manual fallback, reorganized order details split into Details, Services, and Payments tabs, self-service actions (edit profile, access documents, raise support requests), and saved documents for reuse.

Impact: Removed the core manual bottleneck in document handling and gave users visibility into KYC and order status for the first time, while establishing a scalable structure for future account features.


## Simplifying Payment and Protection Decisions in Rentickle

Timeline: 1 Month
Role: UX Researcher, Visual Designer
Tools: Figma, GPT, Miro
Team: Designer: Tanya, Bhavya. Collaborators: Product Managers, Developers

Overview: Advance payment and damage protection stayed hidden until the final checkout step, tied to backend credit checks. Users only learned about deposit deductions after their rental ended, which broke trust, drove cart abandonment, and forced a 24 to 48 hour manual verification delay.

Design Process: Started with an overview of how advance payments and protection plans were working, then mapped where the experience broke down across both flows. Ran competitor research and position mapping against platforms like Cityfurnish, Rentomojo, Furlenco, and Fabrento, tracking user-led control versus manual involvement. Mapped what users were doing, thinking, and feeling at each step to isolate exactly where the system, not the user, was making decisions. Narrowed multiple directions down to three shippable interventions given technical and stakeholder constraints.

What Shipped: Advance payment visibility at the product level, a protection plan surfaced at the cart with full coverage details, and per-item customization instead of applying settings cart-wide.

Impact: Roughly 2x increase in confirmed orders, 82% adoption of the Protection Plan, and 9% adoption of the Advance Plan, lifting total order revenue.


## Reimagining the Checkout Experience

Timeline: 1 Month
Role: UX Researcher, Visual Designer
Tools: Figma, GPT, Clarity
Team: Designer: Tanya (solo). Collaborators: Product Managers, Developers

Overview: Checkout had a 9.8% completion rate and averaged 6 minutes 22 seconds, driven by pricing anxiety, form fatigue, and visual noise. Renting required users to calculate monthly tiers, understand security deposits, and complete mandatory KYC all at once, which paralysed them at the bottom of the funnel.

Design Process: Ran a friction audit using heatmaps and scroll maps (a first hands-on use of this research method) to pinpoint exactly where drop-off occurred, identifying pricing anxiety, form fatigue, and visual noise as the three critical vectors. Mapped user flows and behavioral drop-offs, then redesigned the architecture by removing unnecessary steps and moving secondary actions out of the main path.

What Shipped: A unified, upfront pricing summary showing deposit, monthly fee, and total commitment; the entire checkout consolidated onto a single page (old flow: Cart, Login, Checkout, Address, Agree T&C, Proceed to Pay; new flow: Checkout, Address, Proceed to Payment); and billing address nested inside the shipping address drawer, since only 2% of users needed a separate billing address.

Impact: 48% drop in abandonment at the KYC/verification step, 42% reduction in total checkout duration, and 12% stabilisation in overall completion rates.
